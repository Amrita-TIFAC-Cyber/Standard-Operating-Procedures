## 1. Objective
To streamline and standardize the process for student participation in Hackathons (codeathon, project presentation, ideathon etc) , ensuring academic compliance, quality control, and proper documentation.

## 2. Pre-Application Process (Student Responsibility)

Students intending to participate in a hackathon must follow the steps below:

### Step 1: Initial Request Submission
Students must approach their **Class Advisor** with the following details via the Forms/Excel Sheet as provided by the Hackathon Committee:

- Events Details
    - Event Name
    - Date(s) of the Event
    - Organizing Institution / Platform
    - Mode of Event (Online/Offline/Hybrid)
    - Registration Deadline / Submission Date
    - Event Type (Codeathon, Ideathon, Multithon)
    - State-Level, National or International
- Team Details
    - Team Leader and Team Members (with Roll Numbers)  
    - Idea / Problem Statement to be submitted
    - All Participants from TIFAC-CORE or multidiscplinary teams.
    - Departmental Mentor Details (Mandatory)
    - Any Crucial Details (fees, travel, selection rounds, etc.)

[Link to the Request Submission Form]()

### Step 2: Timeline Requirement
- The request must be submitted **at least 7-15 days prior** (depending on the announcements) to the hackathon submission/registration deadline.

## 3. Class Advisor Review

The Class Advisor will:

- Verify **attendance percentage eligibility**
- Check **On-Duty (OD) limits**
- Evaluate academic standing of the students
- Parents Approval (incase of traveling outside for participation)
- Provide **initial approval or rejection**

If approved:
- The request is **forwarded to the Hackathon Committee via email**.
    - Send an email to Dr. Rohini M (_m_rohini@cb.amrita.edu_) and Mr. Ramaguru Radhakrishnan (_r_ramaguru@cb.amrita.edu_) with Subject **[Request for Approval][Hackathon] Event Name - Team Name** , marked cc to _team members_, _team mentor(s)_ and _class advisor(s)_ of the students.

## 4. Hackathon Committee Review

The Hackathon Committee will:

- Evaluate the **quality and relevance of the hackathon**
- Assess:
  - Reputation of the event and the organising institute/platform
      - Institution
          - Based on their NIRF Ranking/Global Ranking
          - Based on experiences in organising similar prior events
          - Collaborations
      - Event
          - Number of Editions
          - Organized in association with any professional bodies like ACM, IEEE, CSI, etc and Government agencies like Police, MeiTy,              banking.
  - Alignment with department goals
  - Technical Merit of the idea
      - Team's Technical Capability
      - Team's Tech Role Splitup
      - Prototype already available
      - Presented similar idea in any earlier hackathons (mention)
  - Prototype Demo (evaluated along with the Team Mentor(s))
- Provide **final approval or rejection**

### 4.1 Role of the Faculty Mentor

The Team shall choose the Mentor(s) of their chose based on their interest and problem domain aligned with the mentor.

Mentor should:
- Verify the Event details and Team details
- Verify and provide justification for chosing the problem statement (if applicable)
- Verify the proposed Tech Stack with Justification
- Verify the Team Role Splitup
- If the solution proposed is novel, mentor should ensure the IP Rights by contacting the Organisers.
- Verify the Prototype and follow-up meeting before the event
- Identify Alumni or Industry mentors (as and if applicable)
- Verify the final presentation and solution is aligned with Event type and Organising 

## 5. Participation Guidelines

- Students must adhere to institutional rules during participation.
- Any deviation must be reported to the Class Advisor.

## 6. Post-Event Submission (Mandatory)

After participation, the **Team Leader** must submit the following:

- 📸 **Photographic Proof** (team at event)
- 💻 **Prototype Demo** (video / GitHub / presentation)
- 📜 **Certificates of Participation / Achievement**
- 📝 **Completed OD Form**

## 7. Approval and Documentation

### Class Advisor Responsibilities:
- Verify submitted proofs
- Approve OD request
- Include participation in **CRI (Cyber Readiness Index) computation**

### Hackathon Coordinators Responsibilities:
- Approve OD request
- Update **department hackathon records**
    - Potential of the idea
    - Potential of the team
  for future hackathons
- Upload all proofs and documents to **Program Files**

## 8. Important Notes

- Late requests (less than 15 days) **may not be considered**
- Incomplete submissions will lead to **OD rejection**
- Participation without prior approval will **not be eligible for OD**

## 9. Summary Workflow

1. Student → Submit request to Class Advisor  
2. Class Advisor → Verify & forward  
3. Committee → Evaluate & approve  
4. Student → Participate in hackathon  
5. Team Leader → Submit proofs & OD form  
6. Class Advisor → Approve & update CRI  
7. Coordinators → Record & archive
