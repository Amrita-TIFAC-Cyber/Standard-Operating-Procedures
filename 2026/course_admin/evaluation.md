# Standard Operating Procedures - Evaluation Pattern

1. Attendance shall be recorded only for the prescribed instructional hours of the respective courses.

2. Examination, evaluation, viva voce, demonstrations, and other assessment activities shall not be counted towards attendance.

3. One instructional hour shall be equivalent to 50 minutes.

4. The instructional requirements for the category and credit structure of each course are specified in Table 1.

5. The detailed distribution of marks for internal assessments, laboratory evaluations, and end semester examination components for various course categories is presented in Table 2.

6. Students shall satisfy the minimum attendance requirement (**75%**) for each course to become eligible for appearing in the end semester examination.

7. Continuous Assessments (CA) shall be conducted periodically before and after the mid-semester examination as per the academic schedule announced by the department.

8. Mid-semester and end-semester examinations shall be conducted as per the timetable notified by the University.

9. In addition to the regular Continuous Assessments (CA), excluding the Mid-Term Examination, a **Missed Continuous Assessment (Missed-CA)** for theory components and a **Missed Lab Evaluation (Missed-LE)** for laboratory components shall be conducted after announcing the results of CA-2/Lab Evaluation-2 but before the end semester examination. The missed evaluations shall be conducted only in written mode for theory components. The portions and nature of Missed-CA/Missed-LE shall be decided by the faculty and shall be announced two days before the test.

10. Students who were absent for any CA component due to medical reasons (must be approved) may be permitted to appear for Missed-CA or Missed-LE. The marks obtained in the Missed-CA/Missed-LE shall compulsorily replace the missed CA/LE component.

11. Students who have attended all the prescribed Continuous Assessments (CA) shall be permitted to appear only for the Missed-CA (not be eligible to appear for the Missed-LE). In such cases, the marks obtained in the Missed-CA shall compulsorily replace the lowest marks obtained in CAs. The replaced marks shall be treated as final and shall not be discarded, even if the marks obtained in the Missed-CA are lower than the marks originally secured.

12. For Theory–Lab Integrated courses, both the Mid-Semester Examination and the End-Semester Examination shall include a laboratory evaluation component, as specified in Table 2.

13. The laboratory evaluation may be conducted either on the same day as the theory examination scheduled by the Controller of Examinations (COE) or on any instructional/contact day prior to the commencement of the examination. If the course instructor intends to conduct the laboratory evaluation on the same day as the theory examination, prior approval shall be obtained from the COE. Such arrangements shall be communicated before Course Committee Meeting-I, with due intimation to the Department Invigilation Coordinator.

14. Students are expected to maintain academic integrity during all examinations. Any form of malpractice or unfair means shall be dealt with as per university regulations.
