## 1. Purpose
To establish a rigorous, time-bound, and confidential workflow for the preparation, review, and final submission of question papers, ensuring absolute alignment with academic standards and internal compliance mandates.

## 2. Core Pre-requisites & Rules

- **Adherence to Deadlines:** The final deadline announced by the Office of the Deputy Controller of Examinations (DyCoE) is absolute and must be met strictly. No late submissions will be accommodated by the system.

- **The Scrutiny Mandate:** No question paper shall be uploaded or submitted without undergoing a formal review process by an assigned reviewer.

- **Reviewer Assignment:** Faculty members must strictly consult the pre-approved list mapped and issued by the Head of Department (HOD) to identify their designated reviewer.


## 3. Detailed Step-by-Step Workflow

### 3.1 Procure the Scrutiny Form: Phase 1: Initialization

The Course Mentor or Question Paper (QP) Setting Faculty must physically collect the official Scrutiny Form from the Department Office. The faculty must populate the header and preliminary metadata (Course Code, Title, Program, and Date) before proceeding.

### 3.2 Draft the Question Paper and Answer Key: Phase 2: Preparation

Draft the question paper according to the prescribed template, ensuring proper weightage for Bloom's Taxonomy levels.

- **Internal Exams:** Requires one (1) set. Preparing an answer key is strongly recommended.

- **End-Semester Exams:** Requires two (2) distinct sets (Set A and Set B). A detailed, comprehensive Answer Key is mandatory for both sets.

### 3.3 Dispatch to Reviewer: Phase 3: Internal Review

Send the draft question paper(s) to the HOD-approved reviewer via official email at least three (3) working days prior to the final DyCoE submission deadline.

The body of this review request email must explicitly contain:

- Course Code & Course Title
- Planned Date of Final Submission to CMS/DyCoE

### 3.4 The Revision Window: Phase 4: Revision (Proposed Protocol)

The reviewer will evaluate the paper and record feedback directly on the Scrutiny Form.

- **Minor Changes:** Faculty must implement changes within 24 hours.

- **Major Overhaul:** If a paper is rejected or requires deep revision, the faculty member has a maximum turnaround window of 48 hours from receiving feedback to submit the revised draft back to the reviewer for final sign-off.


### 3.5 Final Upload and Submission: Phase 5: Clearance

Once the reviewer signs off on the Scrutiny Form, final delivery is executed based on the exam type:

- **Mid-Term & Internal Evaluations:** Submit exclusively online via the Campus Management System (CMS).

- **End-Semester Examinations:** Upload the digital files to CMS AND submit the physical hard copies of both sets (along with their respective detailed answer keys) in a sealed envelope to the DyCoE office.


### 3.6 Archiving & Compliance: Phase 6: Record Keeping

The fully executed Scrutiny Form (signed by the reviewer) must be safely filed by the course mentor or submitting faculty. These physical forms are properties of academic audits and must be produced immediately upon demand by the HOD, Dean, or external audit committees.


## 4. Summary Matrix for Quick Reference

| Examination Type | Number of Sets Required | Detailed Answer Key | Submission Mode |
|------------------|------------------------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Continuous Assessments | 1 Set | Highly Recommended Not Required | |
| Mid-Term Examination/Lab | 1 Set | Highly Recommended | Online (CMS) Only |
| End-Semester Examination | 2 Sets (Set A & B) | Mandatory for both sets | Dual Mode (Online CMS + Physical Hard Copy) |


> [!WARNING]
> Question papers and answer keys are highly confidential. All digital transmissions between the setter and the reviewer must be password-protected or shared over secure university channels. Never utilize public or unauthorized personal messaging applications to transfer examination materials.
