## 1. Role of Class Advisor & Class Counsellor
#### Class Advisor
The Class Advisor serves as the primary academic mentor and administrative coordinator for a batch of students. The advisor monitors students' academic performance, attendance, and overall progress, ensures compliance with institutional regulations, facilitates communication between students, parents, faculty, and administration, and guides students in academic planning, professional development, and institutional processes.

#### Class Counsellor
The Class Counsellor supports students' personal, behavioral, emotional, and academic well-being. The counsellor regularly monitors student progress and conduct, provides guidance and counselling sessions on a need basis, identifies students requiring additional support, and works closely with the Class Advisor, parents, and faculty members. When necessary, the counsellor recommends students for professional counselling through the institution's designated Central Counselling Services or other authorized support mechanisms as per departmental and institutional guidelines.

> [!NOTE]
> A Class shall have **a Class Advisor and a Class Counsellor**. For Operational Purposes, the students are divided among the Class
> Advisor and Class Counsellor for Mentoring purpose. For that student, the Mentor shall perform the Role of the Class Advisor and Class
> Counsellor. <br/>
> Wherever applicable the Class Advisor mentioned shall be read as Mentor. 

## 2. First Year Class Advisors – Additional Responsibilities (I B.Tech and I M.Tech)

First-year students require structured onboarding and close monitoring. 

### 2.1 Student Onboarding & Coordination
- Coordinate with **Deeksharambh / Swagatham Team** to obtain student details.
- Coordinate with Deeksharambh Team for Orientation Program at Department Level.
- Ensure students have received credentials and access to:
  - University Email ID (@cb.students.amrita.edu)
  - Amrita Univesity Management System (AUMS) 
  - Campus Management System (CMS)
  - MyAmrita Students Portal  
- Follow up to support students receive:
  - ID Cards
  - Uniforms  

### 2.2 Documentation & Verification
- Identify students with **pending document submissions** as per inputs received from Academic Coordination Team via Department Secretary.
- Follow up with the students to submit pending required documents.

### 2.3 Official WhatsApp Channel Creation
- Collect:
  - Student's Official Contact Number
  - Admission category / Auota (slab details)  
- Submit relevant data for **NBA Program Files (P. )**.
- Create Official WhatsApp Group for Class Advisor/Counsellor
- Create a Common WhatsApp Group with All Faculties
- Add the WhatsApp Common Group to TIFAC-CORE Community

### 2.4 Parent & Student Communication
- Inform students about:
  - University policies and regulations  
  - MyAmrita Student Portal/App
  - Hostel Entry Time
  - Dress Code
- Share and maintain:
  - Emergency contact number for the class
- Inform Parents about:
    - MyAmrita Parents Portal/App

### 2.5 Class Committee Formation
- Conduct **Class Representative (CR) elections**.
- Communicate CR details to:
  - Course Instructors within 3 days from the Commencement of Academic sessions.
  - Department Secretary / Administration
- Form a Class Committee with 5-6 students (combination of hostellers, day scholars) 

### 2.6 Initial Academic Orientation
After commencement of classes, brief students on:
- Course Structure and Curriculum
- Course Prerequisites
- Continuous Assessment (CA) and Internal Evaluation  
- Attendance Policy
- Discplinary Rules and Mobile Phone Usage
- Office of Student Affairs (Welfare) and their Privileges
- Examination Procedures

### 2.7 Student Interaction Record (SIR)
Request Department Secretary to procure notebook for Student Interaction Record (SIR). 
Objective of Student Interaction Notebook
- Record Basic Details of the Student
- Feedback about the student based on the classroom activities
- Achievements
- Discplinary Actions

> [!NOTE]
> SIR serves as a main record which any faculty of the department can use to record their observations about any student. This shall be used
> by Director - Cyber Security on need basis.

### 2.8 Data Collection for NBA/NAAC/IQAC
Recommend the Class Advisor to create a email account (gmail) which shall be maintained by Class Advisor/Class Counsellor/Class Representative.
This account shall serve as the central repository to collect and maintain
- Provisional Admission Order (which contains Slab Details)
- MOOC Certificates (NPTEL, Courses, Udemy,...)
- Cyber Security Certifications (EC-Council, CompTIA,...)
- Certificates of Achievements
- Photographs of Achievements
- Professional Bodies Membership Certificate/IDs
- Internship Offer Letters and Completion Certificate
- Placement Offer Letters and Completion Certificate
- Letter of Recommendations (LoR) 
- Higher Studies Notification
- Paper Publication PTP Approval / Certificate / Final Papers
- Final Project Reports
- Alumni Achievements
- Notifications on Discliplinary Actions 

> [!NOTE]
> Tracking and Maintaining these documents are shared responsibility of the Student Mentor and concerned Coordinator.

## 3. General Responsibilities (Applicable to All Years)

### 3.1 Course Registration & Academic Enrollment
- Facilitate **Course Registration** for students from **Semester 2 to Semester 8** at the beginning of each semester.
- Coordinate with the **Department Academic Coordinator** regarding:
  - Regular course registration  
  - Supplementary registration*  
  - Direct 100 registrations*  
  - Course re-registration* (repeat courses)  
- Ensure all students have:
  - Properly registered within deadlines  
  - Course Endrosement is completed as per information received from Academic Coordination Team via Academic Coordinator 
- Verify and maintain records of student registrations for audit purposes.

> [!NOTE]
> Criteria to apply for Supplementary, Direct 100 (D100), Re-registration is as per Amrita School of Computing and University Examination Cell as
> communicated by Academic Coordinator

### 3.2  Academic Monitoring
- Identify students with low attendance and initiate corrective measures.
- Record:
  - Attendance  
  - Internal marks  
  - Academic progress  

### 3.4 Mentoring & Counselling
- Conduct regular counseling sessions.
- Address:
  - Academic challenges  
  - Behavioral concerns  
  - Personal issues (if required)

### 3.5 Mid-Term and End Semester Result Analysis
#### Mid-Term
- Perform post-examination result analysis for Mid-Term Examination within 15 calendar days from the day of last examination.
- Identify:
  - Slow learners  
  - Advanced learners  
- Plan and document corrective actions.
#### End Semester
- Perform post-examination result analysis for End Semester Examination within 5 days from the day of Result Publication.

### 3.6 OD (On-Duty) Tracking
- Monitor participation in:
  - Hackathons  
  - CTF competitions  
  - Technical and non-technical events  
- Maintain proper records for OD approvals.

> [!NOTE]
> As per current directions, a student shall be allowed to take 8 ODs per subject in a Semester. 

### 3.7 Student Development Tracking
- Track:
  - Awards and recognitions  
  - Co-curricular and extracurricular activities  
- Update these activities and records in:
  - MyAmrita Portal under Awards Portal
  - Student Interaction Record (SIR)

### 3.8 Institutional & Club Activities
- Encourage participation in:
  - Department Events  
  - School/University events  
  - Technical Clubs and Professional Bodies  
- Track student involvement.

### 3.9 Continuous Comprehensive Monitoring (CCM)
- Conduct at least **2 CCM reviews per semester**.
- Document performance, feedback, and action plans.

## 4. Final Year Class Advisors – Additional Responsibilities (IV B.Tech and II M.Tech)

Final-year students require focused career and academic completion support.

### 4.1 Placement Support
- Monitor student participation in:
  - Placement drives  
  - Internship opportunities  
- Ensure all eligible students are actively engaged.

### 4.2 Higher Studies Guidance
- Identify and guide students for:
  - Higher education (M.Tech/MS/PhD)  
  - Competitive examinations  

### 4.3 Research & Publications
- Encourage and track:
  - Paper publications
      - Record PTP ID, Paper Acceptance Copy, Plag Report, Presentation Certificate and Publication Link
  - Final Year Projects  
  - Internship certifications  

### 4.4 Distinction & Awards Tracking
- Identify students eligible for:
  - Distinction  
  - Academic awards  

### 4.5 Academic Completion
- Ensure:
  - Completion of all course credits  
  - No pending academic requirements  

### 4.6 Student Recommendations
- Provide recommendations for:
  - Placements  
  - Higher studies  
  - External opportunities
