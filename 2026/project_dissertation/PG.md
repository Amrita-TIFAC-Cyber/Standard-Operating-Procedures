### 1. Guidelines

#### 1.1 General

1. A Dissertation Committee consisting of the Director, TIFAC-CORE in Cyber Security and delegated faculty members shall oversee all aspects of the M Tech dissertation.
2. Allocation of the Dissertation Advisor (hereupon referred to as the Advisor) is based on the procedure drafted by the Centre.
3. Each dissertation shall have a physical Log Book and a digital Project Progress Tracker excel document, where the progress of the dissertation work will be recorded periodically.
4. A Proposal (Zeroth) Review shall be conducted by the Dissertation Committee...
5. The student shall maintain and fill out the Project Progress Tracker...
6. Request for change in dissertation topic will not be entertained after second review.
7. Students shall address the suggestions mentioned in the log book after each review.
8. Advisors shall monitor the attendance of the students regularly.
9. In the case of students who have taken up internship...
10. Towards completion of the dissertation, the student is required to submit two hard copies of dissertation report along with a soft copy to the Advisor.
11. The eligibility of the students to appear for the Final Viva Voce Examination...

#### 1.2 Research Paper Publication

12. The Advisor must ensure that the student carries sufficient research work...
13. A research paper describes the motivation and significance...
14. The Advisors are responsible for checking the authenticity...
15. The student must prepare the manuscript on his/her own...
16. All publications based upon the research work should list the name of the Advisor...
17. All research papers submitted for publication should be routed through Publication Review Committee.
18. Submission of the research paper should be made by the Advisor only after obtaining approval.
19. Subsequently, on approval, the copy of the research paper must be uploaded to PTP.

### 2. Program Objectives

* Technical Depth
* Practical Implementation
* Research Rigor
* Communication Excellence

### 3. Timeline and Milestones

#### Month 0: Problem Identification and Literature Review

* At least 2 hours per week with the Advisor.
* Communicate to Advisor and Co-advisor at least 3 working days before review.
* Report milestone completion and eligibility.
* Upload documents to OneDrive.

**Review PPT must contain:**

* Title Page
* Tentative title
* Proposal Review subtitle
* Student details
* Guide details
* Literature Survey
* Problem Statement
* Requirements and Challenges
* Expected Outcome

**1-Page Write-up**

* Tentative project title
* Justification
* Annotated bibliography
* Research questions
* Feasibility requirements

#### Proposal Review

**Evaluation Criteria**

* Literature Quality
* Research Gap Identification
* Problem Statement
* Problem Relevance
* Originality/Publishability
* Feasibility
* Expected Outcomes

#### Month 1: Methodology and System Design

* Mandatory attendance.
* Minimum 2 contact hours per week.
* Define project methodology.
* Architecture design.
* Evaluation plan.
* Gantt chart.
* Risk assessment.
* Progress Tracker updates.
* Review PPT submission.
* Code repository.
* Thesis draft.

**First Review Evaluation**

* Methodological Soundness (40%)
* Technical Feasibility (35%)
* Innovation (25%)

#### Month 2–3: Implementation Phase I

* Progress Tracker update.
* 50% working prototype.
* Progress report.
* Dissertation draft.
* Repository updates.

**Second and Third Review**

* Implementation Progress (50%)
* Code Quality (30%)
* Problem-solving Adaptability (20%)

#### Month 4: Implementation Phase II and Testing

* Complete implementation.
* Testing and benchmarking.
* Comparative analysis.
* Updated thesis draft.

**Fourth Review**

* Completeness (40%)
* Testing Rigor (35%)
* Performance Benchmarking (25%)

#### Month 5: Documentation and Final Review

* Final dissertation draft.
* IEEE/ACM format.
* 50–80 pages.
* GitHub repository.
* Final presentation and demo.

### 4. Student Responsibilities

#### Monthly Requirements

* Minimum 2 contact hours per week.
* Submit deliverables 3 days before review.
* Maintain dissertation journal.
* Attend meetings.
* Attend seminar series.

#### Communication Protocol

1. Respond within 24–48 hours.
2. Escalate emergencies after 72 hours.
3. Use institutional communication channels.

## Coordinator

### Pre-Semester Actions

* Prepare Course Plan.
* Prepare CO, CO-PO and CO-BTL mappings.
* Prepare Rubrics.
* Prepare student lists.
* Prepare attendance registers.
* Prepare Progress Tracker.
* Prepare Review Log Book.
* Prepare LaTeX template.
* Prepare PPT template.
* Prepare review schedule.
* Assess academic calendar.
* Plan evaluation mode.

### Before Each Review

* Verify student eligibility.
* Verify attendance.
* Verify advisor approval.
* Finalize review panel.
* Share project descriptions.
* Schedule reviews.
* Share rubrics.
* Arrange venue and infrastructure.

### After Each Review

* Verify Progress Tracker updates.
* Verify Log Book completion.
* Consolidate marks.
* Digitize review marks.
* Identify at-risk students.
* Conduct remediation meetings.
* Maintain academic records.

### After Third Review

* Identify at-risk students.
* Publish interim grades.
* Consolidate internal marks.

### After Final Review

* Finalize external review panel.
* Collect deliverables.
* Verify compliance.
* Publish results.

### Quality Benchmarks

* 85% on-time milestone completion.
* Maximum 2-week delay per milestone.
* Formal remediation after 4-week delay.

### Guide Change

* Permitted only once before Review 2.
* Requires Dean approval.

### Topic Change

* Permitted only before Review 2.
* Requires fresh Proposal Review.

### Extensions

* Maximum 2-week delay with approval.
* 4-week cumulative delay triggers probation.
