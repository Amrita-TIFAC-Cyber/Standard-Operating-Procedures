## Purpose 
To ensure that publication requirements are administered separately from regular course assessments, maintain fairness and consistency in student evaluation, encourage voluntary research engagement, and provide a standardized mechanism for implementing publication-related provisions applicable to distinction eligibility, publication credits, and grade improvement in accordance with the prescribed University policy.

## Standard Guidelines 

1.	Students seeking eligibility for the award of distinction at the time of graduation must have submitted or published one research paper, in addition to fulfilling all other prescribed academic requirements. 
2.	All publications must be in Scopus-indexed journals/conferences and shall comply with the guidelines prescribed by Amrita Vishwa Vidyapeetham. 
3.	In every publication considered under this policy, the concerned faculty member/Project Guide shall be the Corresponding Author, and the institutional affiliation of the student author shall be Amrita Vishwa Vidyapeetham.
4.	Each student author shall be awarded 10 marks for every publication in which he/she is an author or co-author, provided the paper is accepted in Scopus/PubMed-indexed Tier 1 or Tier 2 journals. 
5.	For all other Scopus/PubMed-indexed journals and conferences, the 10 marks shall be distributed among the student authors based on their individual contribution. 
6.	Publication marks shall be added after the end-semester examinations in any subject where the student is eligible for grade improvement. 
7.	For students with a CGPA of 8.00 or above, the first paper shall be considered for eligibility for distinction, and the second paper, if any, shall be considered for publication marks as per Point 6. 
8.	For students with a CGPA below 8.00, the first paper shall be considered for improvement of the grade, and the second paper, if any, shall be considered for eligibility for distinction. If the CGPA remains below 8.00 even after adding publication marks, the publications shall be utilized only improvement of the grade and the student will not be eligible for distinction.
