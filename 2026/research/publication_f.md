## Purpose 
To ensure that publication requirements are administered separately from regular course assessments, maintain fairness and consistency in student evaluation, encourage voluntary research engagement, and provide a standardized mechanism for implementing publication-related provisions applicable to distinction eligibility, publication credits, and grade improvement in accordance with the prescribed University policy.

## Standard Guidelines 

1.	The publication requirement prescribed by the University shall be considered as a separate academic criterion for eligibility for the Distinction and shall not form part of the regular assessment component of any course. 
2.	Research publications shall not be made a mandatory requirement under Internal Assessment (including Continuous Assessment and Mid-Semester Assessment) or under any other regular evaluation component of any course. 
3.	No student shall be compelled to publish a paper for the purpose of securing marks in assignments, seminars, laboratory courses, projects, or continuous evaluation components, except where publication-related outcomes are specifically approved as part of the Final Year Project component or formally notified curriculum requirements. 
4.	Publications may be encouraged to promote academic enrichment, innovation, and research exposure. However, engagement in publication-related activities shall remain voluntary unless specifically provided for under approved curriculum regulations. 
5.	The consideration of publications, wherever applicable, shall be strictly governed by policy **Amrita/CBE/TIFAC/2026-27/Research/Student-Pub-01**, relating to eligibility for the Distinction, publication credits, and provisions for the improvement of grade.
